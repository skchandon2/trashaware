<h2>Garbage sorting guide</h2>

<p>Be aware that sorting waste at home is different than on-campus - UW waste programs accept different items. Please consult this sorting guide when you are unsure if an item can be placed into the garbage stream.</p>

<p>Certain items may be suitable for donation* </p>

<div class="expandable">
	<h2>BICYCLES*</h2>

	<div class="expandable-content">
		<p>Bicycles are not recyclable but are suitable for donation. There are a few options for donating your bicycle; you can visit <a href="https://www.theworkingcentre.org/recycle-cycles/153">Recycle Cycles</a> run by the Working Centre, or visit the Region of Waterloo's <a href="https://www.regionofwaterloo.ca/en/living-here/residential-reduce-and-reuse-options.aspx">Residential Reduce and Reuse Options page</a> &gt; Drop-off facilities and programs for residents &gt; Drop-off programs operated by the Region &gt; Bicycles for more information on their bicycle reuse and refurbishment program. </p>

		<p>Otherwise, bicycles are to be disposed into the Garbage stream.</p>
	</div>
</div>

<div class="expandable">
	<h2>CANDY WRAPPERS</h2>

	<div class="expandable-content">
		<p>Candy wrappers are <b>not recyclable</b> and should be placed in the Garbage (black) receptacles. </p>
	</div>
</div>

<div class="expandable">
	<h2>FOILS (SOILED)</h2>

	<div class="expandable-content">

	</div>
</div>

<div class="expandable">
	<h2>JEWELRY*</h2>

	<div class="expandable-content">
		<p>Jewelry is not recyclable but is suitable for donation. Visit the Region of Waterloo's <a href="https://www.regionofwaterloo.ca/en/living-here/residential-reduce-and-reuse-options.aspx">Residential Reduce and Reuse Options page</a> &gt; Directory of local programs, businesses and organizations &gt; Jewellery for a list of donation locations. </p>

<div class="expandable">

	</div>
</div>

<div class="expandable">
	<h2>PLASTIC BAGS</h2>

	<div class="expandable-content">
		<p><strong>On campus:</strong> Plastic bags (e.g. shopping bags, bread bags, Ziploc bags) cannot be recycled on campus and should be placed in the Garbage (black) receptacles.</p>

		<p><b>At home: </b>Plastic bags can be recycled through the Region of Waterloo's curbside collection and can be placed in your Paper and Bags recycling bins. If you have multiple bags, please place them all into one bag and place them in the bin.</p>
	</div>
</div>

<div class="expandable">

<div class="expandable">
	<h2>PLASTIC WRAPPING</h2>

	<div class="expandable-content">

		<p><b>At home:</b> The Region of Waterloo collects soft plastic wrapping with other plastic bags in the Paper and Bags bin through curbside recycling. </p>
